Honeysuckle

Honeysuckle is a nostalgic flowering shrub that is most famous for its sweet, dreamsicle-like scent. The flowers are a beautiful light orange and white color and are attractive to pollinators and hummingbirds.

Honeysuckle is a hardy shrub that blooms in late spring through mid-summer. It is suitable for USDA Hardiness Zones 5 to 9 and prefers full sun exposure. Some varieties of Honeysuckle can be invasive, so it's important to choose the right one for your garden.

Arborvitae

Arborvitae is a popular shrub used for privacy screening. It is hardy and can tolerate a wide range of soil conditions. To prepare Arborvitae, prune it in the early spring to maintain its shape and size.

Azalea

Azalea is a beautiful flowering shrub that is perfect for adding a pop of color to your garden. It is sensitive to heat and requires partial shade. To prepare Azalea, prune it after flowering to remove any dead or damaged branches.
